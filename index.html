Top Destinations in El Salvador

El Tunco

A surfer's paradise known for its black sand beaches and vibrant nightlife.

Cerro El Pital

Cerro El Pital is a mountain located in Central America, straddling the border between El Salvador and Honduras. Rising to an elevation of 2,730 meters (8,957 feet) above sea level, it stands as the highest point in El Salvador and the third highest in Honduras. Situated just 12 kilometers (7 miles) from the town of La Palma, Cerro El Pital is nestled within a lush cloud forest, offering a unique and misty natural environment.

El Tazumal

The Tazumal archaeological site is located in the municipality of Chalchuapa, in the department of Santa Ana, El Salvador, approximately 80 kilometers west of the capital city. It lies within the broader Chalchuapa archaeological zone, which spans roughly 10 square kilometers and also includes other important sites such as Peñate, Casa Blanca, El Trapiche, and Las Victorias.

Santa Ana Volcano (Ilamatepec)

The Santa Ana Volcano, also known as Ilamatepec (Spanish: volcán de Santa Ana), is a prominent stratovolcano situated in the Santa Ana department of El Salvador. Standing at 2,381 meters (7,812 feet) above sea level, it holds the distinction of being the highest volcano in the country. The volcano is located just west of the Coatepeque Caldera.
