El Tazumal

El Tazumal is one of El Salvador’s most important archaeological sites, offering a fascinating glimpse into ancient Mayan civilization.

1. Ancient Mayan City

Walk where Mayan priests and astronomers once stood. The site dates back over 1,000 years and includes ceremonial altars, pyramids, and tombs.

2. Massive Pyramid

The main structure rises over 24 meters and showcases layered construction, ritual altars, and original steps.

3. Spiritual Energy

  • Offerings to Mayan gods
  • Ritual spaces for sacred events
  • A celestial observatory

4. Cultural Connection

Artifacts from Guatemala, Mexico, and Honduras reveal how connected this city was in Mesoamerican trade.

5. Onsite Museum

The Stanley Boggs Museum displays figurines, stone idols, ceremonial tools, and burial artifacts—enhancing your visit.

6. Easy Day Trip

  • Located in Chalchuapa, 1.5 hours from San Salvador
  • Nearby markets, pupuserías, and colonial landmarks
